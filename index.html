Audiora

AI-Powered Wearable Sound Awareness System

Enhancing safety and independence for individuals with hearing loss through real-time environmental sound recognition and directional feedback.

The Problem

Over 1.5 billion people worldwide experience hearing loss, according to the World Health Organization.

Most assistive technologies primarily focus on speech recognition.

Environmental sound detection and localization remain largely unaddressed.

Failing to recognize warning sounds such as sirens, vehicle horns, or explosions creates serious safety risks and limits independent living.

The Solution – What is Audiora?

Audiora is a wearable AI-based system that transforms environmental sound into accessible real-time feedback.

🔊

Sound Recognition

Detects environmental risk-related sounds.

📍

Direction Detection

Identifies sound source location (left, right, center).

📳

Multimodal Alerts

Provides visual and vibration feedback.

⚡

Real-Time Processing

Operates with ~150 ms update intervals.

Meet the Founder

Zerya Cimen

Audiora was founded by Zerya Cimen, a student innovator focused on AI-driven assistive technologies. Motivated by the safety challenges faced by individuals with hearing loss, she developed Audiora to bridge the gap between environmental awareness and wearable accessibility.

Her work combines embedded systems, TinyML, and human-centered design to create practical, real-world impact.

How It Works (Technical Overview)

Audiora integrates embedded AI and directional audio processing into a compact wearable system.

Dual microphones capture stereo environmental audio

A TinyML model classifies sound types in real time

Inter-channel time delay estimates sound direction

Alerts activate only above a 0.55 confidence threshold

Dynamic gain normalization stabilizes signal amplitude
